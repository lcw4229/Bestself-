─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I.  LIABILITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

On November 6, 2025, Yudan Huang was operating her vehicle in a lawful and
prudent manner when Melvin Louis Morrow, operating a Mack truck, negligently
and without warning rear-ended Ms. Huang's vehicle with significant force.
The violent impact caused Ms. Huang to be thrown forward and back within her
vehicle, causing immediate and severe injuries to her cervical and lumbar
spine.

The collision was investigated and documented in Police Report No. TD0976.
Liability in this matter is clear and uncontested — your insured drove a
large commercial Mack truck directly into the rear of Ms. Huang's vehicle.
Under Texas law, a rear-end collision gives rise to a presumption of
negligence on the part of the following driver. Your insured failed to
maintain a safe following distance, failed to keep a proper lookout, and
failed to control his vehicle — all in violation of the Texas Transportation
Code.

II.  INJURIES AND MEDICAL TREATMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A.  Initial Emergency Evaluation — Plano Supreme Primary Care
    Dr. Yinghui Liu, M.D. | November 7, 2025

The day after the collision, Ms. Huang presented to Plano Supreme Primary
Care, where she was evaluated by Qian Su, NP under the supervision of
Dr. Yinghui Liu, M.D. Ms. Huang reported immediate onset of neck pain, low
back pain, and head pain following the crash. On examination, she demonstrated
tenderness and pain consistent with acute traumatic injury.

Cervical and lumbar x-rays were ordered and revealed:

    •   Mild degenerative disc disease at C5-6;
    •   Facet arthropathy at C7-T1;
    •   Degenerative disc disease at L4-5 and L5-S1; and
    •   Lumbar facet arthropathy.

Ms. Huang was diagnosed with the following:

    •   Motor vehicle accident (ICD-10: V89.2XXA);
    •   Acute neck pain / cervical strain (ICD-10: M54.2);
    •   Low back pain / lumbar strain (ICD-10: M54.50); and
    •   Concussion without loss of consciousness (ICD-10: S06.0X0A).

She was prescribed Tizanidine HCl 2 mg for muscle spasm and referred for
further evaluation and imaging.

B.  Diagnostic Imaging — MRI Centers of Texas / Uptown Radiology Associates
    Dr. Chad Porter, M.D. | November 22, 2025

On November 22, 2025, Ms. Huang underwent MRI studies of both the cervical
and lumbar spine. The studies were interpreted by Dr. Chad Porter, M.D., and
revealed objective, traumatically caused structural injuries:

    CERVICAL SPINE MRI FINDINGS:

    •   C5-C6: Grade 1 retrolisthesis with a 2.2 mm posterior disc herniation
        encroaching on the anterior thecal sac — the most clinically
        significant finding, consistent with the acute traumatic mechanism of
        a rear-end impact.

    LUMBAR SPINE MRI FINDINGS:

    •   L4-L5: 2.2 mm posterior disc herniation with thecal sac impression;
    •   L3-L4: 1.5 mm posterior disc herniation.

These MRI findings confirm structural disc injuries at multiple cervical and
lumbar levels — injuries that are consistent with, and caused by, the violent
rear-end impact inflicted by your insured.

C.  Spine Specialist Care — Momentum Spine & Joint
    Dr. Zeshan Chaudhry, M.D. & Dr. Kamlesh Sisodiya, M.D.
    November 17, 2025 – January 21, 2026

Ms. Huang was evaluated at Momentum Spine & Joint on three occasions by
board-certified spine specialists. Dr. Zeshan Chaudhry, M.D. performed her
initial evaluation on November 17, 2025. Follow-up care was provided by
Dr. Kamlesh Sisodiya, M.D. on December 1, 2025 and January 21, 2026.

The treating physicians reviewed Ms. Huang's MRI studies and clinical
presentation and confirmed the diagnosis of traumatic cervical and lumbar disc
herniations. Based upon her clinical course and failure to achieve complete
resolution through conservative care alone, the physicians determined that
Ms. Huang will require the following interventional procedures:

    CERVICAL SPINE:
    •   Bilateral Medial Branch Block (MBB), C4-C5 / C5-C6
    •   Bilateral Radiofrequency Thermal Coagulation (RFTC), C4-C5 / C5-C6

    LUMBAR SPINE:
    •   Bilateral Medial Branch Block (MBB), L4-L5 / L5-S1
    •   Bilateral Radiofrequency Thermal Coagulation (RFTC), L4-L5 / L5-S1

Procedure cost estimates for all four recommended interventions are enclosed
herewith. These are not elective — they represent the medically necessary
next step in Ms. Huang's treatment as determined by her treating spine
specialists.

D.  Prescription Medications — ASP Cares Pharmacy
    November 17, 2025

Ms. Huang's treating physicians prescribed the following medications to manage
her pain and inflammation:

    •   Lidocaine 5% Topical Patch; and
    •   Meloxicam 7.5 mg (anti-inflammatory).

E.  Physical Therapy — ATI Physical Therapy
    November 26, 2025 – February 27, 2026

Ms. Huang completed a course of physical therapy at ATI Physical Therapy,
attending twenty-two (22) sessions over a three-month period from
November 26, 2025 through February 27, 2026.

At her initial evaluation, Ms. Huang scored 46% on the Neck Disability Index
(NDI), placing her in the "Significant Disability" category. She reported
difficulty performing her work duties, managing household tasks, engaging in
sustained activities, and sleeping — all direct consequences of the injuries
inflicted by your insured.

Through diligent participation in her rehabilitation program, Ms. Huang
improved to a 24% NDI score at discharge on February 23, 2026. While she
achieved functional improvement through physical therapy, the structural disc
injuries identified on MRI persist and continue to require the interventional
spine procedures recommended by her physicians. Ms. Huang endured months of
pain, limited function, and disruption to her daily life — all of which were
caused solely by the negligence of Melvin Louis Morrow.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Beyond her quantifiable economic losses, Ms. Huang has suffered and continues
to suffer substantial non-economic damages. She endured months of acute pain
in her neck and back following a traumatic rear-end collision with a large
commercial Mack truck. She suffered from disrupted sleep, an inability to
perform normal household tasks, and significant difficulty meeting her work
and daily obligations. She underwent months of physical therapy, multiple
physician visits, diagnostic imaging, and pharmacological treatment — all
while carrying the burden of structural spine injuries that have permanently
altered the trajectory of her health.

The persistent disc herniations at C5-C6, L3-L4, and L4-L5 — confirmed by
MRI — will require invasive interventional procedures. The pain, anxiety,
and loss of enjoyment of life that Ms. Huang has experienced and will
continue to experience are real, significant, and compensable.

In light of the nature and severity of her injuries, Ms. Huang's general
damages — including past and future physical pain and suffering, mental
anguish, and loss of enjoyment of life — are substantial and, when combined
with her special damages, result in a total damages exposure that greatly
exceeds your insured's policy limits.

V.  STOWERS DEMAND
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pursuant to the holding in G.A. Stowers Furniture Co. v. American Indemnity
Co., 15 S.W.2d 544 (Tex. Comm'n App. 1929, holding approved), a liability
insurer has a duty to exercise ordinary care in deciding whether to settle a
claim within policy limits when a reasonable prudent insurer would do so. That
duty is triggered when: (1) the claim against the insured is within the scope
of coverage; (2) demand is made within policy limits; and (3) the terms of
the demand are such that a reasonably prudent insurer would accept. All three
conditions are plainly met here.

Ms. Huang's total documented and anticipated damages — past medical expenses
of $25,558.58, future medical expenses of $100,973.50, and substantial general
damages for pain, suffering, and mental anguish — far exceed the applicable
policy limits of your insured, Melvin Louis Morrow.

Accordingly, we hereby make formal demand upon Acuity, A Mutual Insurance
Company, for the full available bodily injury liability limits under all
applicable policies insuring Melvin Louis Morrow in connection with the
November 6, 2025 collision — in the amount of $[POLICY LIMITS].

This demand will remain open for thirty (30) days from the date of receipt
of this letter, expiring no later than [INSERT DEADLINE DATE — 30 DAYS FROM
RECEIPT]. This demand is unconditional, requires no release of any party
other than Melvin Louis Morrow, and requires only a full tender of the
applicable policy limits.

Should you fail to tender the policy limits within the time provided, Acuity
will have breached its duty to its insured under Stowers and its progeny,
including American Physicians Ins. Exchange v. Garcia, 876 S.W.2d 842
(Tex. 1994). Acuity will then be exposed to liability for any judgment
rendered against its insured in excess of the policy limits, including any
post-judgment interest. We urge you in the strongest possible terms to protect
your insured by accepting this demand before the deadline expires.
